## Full funnel journey

### Step 1 — Cold outreach

**Channel:** LinkedIn DM + cold email (existing growth playbook)

**Role:** First touch. Establish credibility and surface a specific pain point relevant to the recipient's archetype. The message does not pitch SBE. It offers the free toolkit as something useful on its own terms.

**Copy anchor per archetype:**
- Ops Manager → "free state-by-state RSA audit checklist"
- Owner-Operator → "free RSA log + onboarding SOP bundle"
- Venue Manager → "free staff onboarding SOP templates"

**Measure:** Reply rate, click-through to landing page

**Signal we're after:** Any click to the landing page indicates sufficient pain/curiosity to proceed

---

### Step 2 — Landing page

**Role:** Convert interest to email capture. The page has one job: make the toolkit feel immediately useful, and make the cost of not having it feel real. No SBE pitch on this page.

**Capture fields:** First name, email, venue type (dropdown: independent venue / group / venue manager)

**Offer framing:** "Free Notion compliance and onboarding toolkit for Australian hospitality venues"

**Measure:** Visitor-to-capture conversion rate (target: 35%+)

**Signal we're after:** Email address + venue type. Venue type determines which pain narrative is dominant in the nurture sequence.

---

### Step 3 — Toolkit delivery (Email 1)

**Sent:** Immediately on capture

**Role:** Deliver the Notion link. Set the usage frame: "This is a working document for your venue — not a guide to read, a tool to fill out." Open a curiosity thread about what they'll find when they complete it.

**No pitch in this email.**

**Measure:** Email open rate, Notion link click rate

**Signal we're after:** Notion link click confirms toolkit engagement has started

---

### Step 4 — Toolkit use (Notion asset)

**Role:** The trojan horse. The act of completing the templates surfaces the manual burden. Friction counters accumulate time costs. Gap callouts name compliance risk. The resolution block introduces SBE once, plainly, at the end.

**Measure:** Notion page views (via Potion/Super or a redirect link with UTM tracking); CTA link clicks from within the toolkit

**Signal we're after:** A click on the resolution block CTA from within Notion is the highest-intent signal in the funnel — this person has felt the pain and self-identified

---

### Step 5 — Nurture sequence (Emails 2–4)

**Email 2 — RSA pain** (Day 3)
- Hook: "If a licensing officer walked in tonight, what would you hand them?"
- Amplifies compliance fear using the specific scenario language from Profile 1/2
- References the incident log they have in hand
- Soft SBE mention — one sentence, no CTA

**Email 3 — Onboarding pain** (Day 6)
- Hook: Australian hospitality turnover rate as the opening fact (70–80% annual)
- The onboarding cost angle — connects to the time-cost formula in the SOP template
- References the SOP they have in hand
- Soft SBE mention — one sentence, no CTA

**Email 4 — Conversion** (Day 9)
- Hook: "You've now got the manual system. Here's why most venues quietly stop using it after three weeks."
- The founding member rate: $49/month, locked for life, future rate $79
- Hard CTA: "Lock in your founding rate → servebyexample.co/pricing"
- Urgency: limited founding member spots, month-to-month, cancel anytime

**Measure:** Open rate per email, CTA click rate on Email 4

**Signal we're after:** Email 4 CTA click = conversion-ready; waitlist/checkout completion = converted

---

### Step 6 — Waitlist / founding rate conversion

**Destination:** servebyexample.co/pricing → Single Venue plan at AUD $49/month

**Role:** Close. The pricing page already carries the founding rate narrative and the urgency frame. Nothing new needs to be added here.

**Measure:** Waitlist signups, paid conversions, MRR contribution

---

## Measurement summary

| Step | Primary metric | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Cold outreach | Click-through to landing page | 5–8% |
| Landing page | Email capture rate | 35%+ |
| Email 1 | Notion link click rate | 60%+ |
| Toolkit (Notion) | Resolution block CTA click | 10–15% of openers |
| Email 2–3 | Open rate | 45%+ |
| Email 4 | CTA click rate | 20%+ |
| Pricing page | Waitlist / paid conversion | 15–25% of clickers |

---

## What this funnel is not

- It is not a content marketing play. There is no SEO, no social amplification, no viral sharing assumption.
- It is not a drip course or value-delivery nurture. Emails 2 and 3 are pain amplifiers, not educational content.
- It is not a free-trial funnel. The toolkit delivers standalone value — but it is engineered to make the manual process feel expensive, not to give SBE away for free.
