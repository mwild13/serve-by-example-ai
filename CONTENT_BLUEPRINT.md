## CATEGORY 1: TECHNICAL SKILLS (7 Modules)

### MODULE 1: Pouring the Perfect Beer
- **ID:** 1
- **Category:** Technical
- **Difficulty:** 2/5
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Description:** Master beer pouring techniques—angle, head ratio, temperature, glassware matching.
- **Target Learner:** Bartender, Waiter, Bar Back
- **Scenarios Count:** 12 (3 L1, 3 L2, 3 L3, 3 L4)

#### Level 1: Rapid-Fire Quiz (Recall)
1. **Q:** What is the ideal head ratio for a beer pour?  
   **A:** 1-1.5 inches (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Proper head ratio releases carbonation and enhances flavor, while allowing full glass volume.

2. **Q:** At what angle should you pour a beer to minimize foam?  
   **A:** 45 degrees initially, then straighten to 90 degrees (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Starting at 45° reduces turbulence; straightening builds proper head.

3. **Q:** What is the standard beer serving temperature?  
   **A:** 38-55°F depending on style (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Lagers 38-50°F, Ales 50-55°F to preserve flavor compounds.

#### Level 2: Descriptor Selection (Pick 2 of 5)
1. **Prompt:** A customer orders a stout. Which descriptors apply to proper stout pouring?  
   **Correct:** "Slower pour to minimize overflow," "1.5-inch head to trap aromatics"  
   **Incorrect:** "Fast aggressive pour," "Icy cold glassware," "Minimal head for full glass"

2. **Prompt:** When pouring a lager, which are correct?  
   **Correct:** "45° angle to reduce turbulence," "Serve at 38-45°F"  
   **Incorrect:** "At room temperature," "Maximum head ratio," "Vertical pour only"

3. **Prompt:** A guest complains their beer is too foamy. What went wrong?  
   **Correct:** "Glass was dirty or wet," "Pour was too aggressive"  
   **Incorrect:** "Beer was too cold," "Head ratio was correct," "Angle was too shallow"

#### Level 3: Advanced Descriptors (Pick 3 of 5, Shuffled)
1. **Prompt:** Troubleshoot: A beer pours with excessive head that won't settle. What are three factors?  
   **Correct (any 3):** "Dirty glassware," "Over-carbonated keg," "Pour angle too aggressive," "Temperature too warm"  
   **Incorrect:** "Beer style (stout, lager, IPA)," "Tap line too long"

2. **Prompt:** A craft IPA should be poured to maximize hop aromatics. Which three apply?  
   **Correct (any 3):** "Tilt glass 45° to release CO2 gradually," "Serve at 50-55°F," "1-1.25 inch head," "Pour into clean, dry glass"  
   **Incorrect:** "Chill to near-freezing," "Minimal head," "Aggressive pour"

3. **Prompt:** For consistent beer pours across your bar, which three practices matter most?  
   **Correct (any 3):** "Train all staff on angle/technique," "Clean tap lines weekly," "Maintain proper keg pressure," "Check glass cleanliness"  
   **Incorrect:** "All beers served at same temp," "Always use same glassware," "Fast pouring saves time"

#### Level 4: Roleplay Scenarios (Open-Ended, AI-Evaluated)
1. **Scenario:** A guest orders their third beer but complains the first two had too much head. Walk through how you'd pour their next one, explaining each step.  
   **Evaluation Dimensions:**
   - Communication: Does the bartender explain the technique clearly?
   - Problem-Solving: Do they diagnose the previous issue (glass cleanliness, pressure, angle)?
   - Attention to Detail: Do they mention glass inspection, temperature, angle adjustment?
   - Professionalism: Tone and willingness to make it right.

2. **Scenario:** A busy Friday night—multiple beer orders at once. Describe your pouring sequence to maintain quality while managing speed.  
   **Evaluation:** Time management, consistency, safe handling, communication with other staff.

3. **Scenario:** A customer orders "a good pour" of an IPA they've never tried. Walk them through what makes it special.  
   **Evaluation:** Product knowledge, enthusiasm, technique explanation, guest education.

### MODULE 2: Wine Knowledge & Service
- **ID:** 2
- **Category:** Technical
- **Difficulty:** 3/5
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Description:** Wine classification, tasting notes, pairing, service temperature, proper pouring, upselling.
- **Target Learner:** Bartender, Waiter, Manager
- **Scenarios Count:** 15 (4 L1, 4 L2, 4 L3, 3 L4)

#### Level 1: Rapid-Fire Quiz (Recall)
1. **Q:** What is the proper serving temperature for white wine?  
   **A:** 45-50°F (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Preserves acidity and floral notes while preventing numbing from extreme cold.

2. **Q:** Which wine region is known for Pinot Noir?  
   **A:** Burgundy, France OR Willamette Valley, Oregon (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Burgundy is historic birthplace; modern Oregonian Pinots rival it.

3. **Q:** What does "dry" mean in wine terminology?  
   **A:** Low residual sugar content (correct)  
   **Explanation:** All natural sugars converted to alcohol during fermentation.

4. **Q:** What is the proper angle for pouring wine into a glass?  
   **A:** 45-60 degrees to avoid foam and spills (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Prevents splashing and preserves wine integrity.

#### Level 2: Descriptor Selection (Pick 2 of 5)
1. **Prompt:** A guest orders a light, crisp white for hot weather. Which descriptors match?  
   **Correct:** "Sauvignon Blanc," "Pinot Grigio"  
   **Incorrect:** "Cabernet Sauvignon," "Oaked Chardonnay," "Fortified wine"

2. **Prompt:** When serving red wine, which practices are correct?  
   **Correct:** "Serve at 60-65°F," "Allow wine to breathe 10-15 min if needed"  
   **Incorrect:** "Serve chilled like white wine," "Cork must be synthetic," "Always decant before serving"

3. **Prompt:** A customer wants wine that pairs with grilled salmon. Which work?  
   **Correct:** "Pinot Noir," "Sauvignon Blanc"  
   **Incorrect:** "Cabernet Sauvignon," "Port wine," "Rosé only"

4. **Prompt:** Which are signs of proper wine service at your table?  
   **Correct:** "Present bottle label to guest," "Pour from guest's right side"  
   **Incorrect:** "Fill glass completely full," "Pour for ladies first always," "Never ask if guest wants more"

#### Level 3: Advanced Descriptors (Pick 3 of 5)
1. **Prompt:** An Australian Shiraz at $40/bottle appeals to which three guest profiles?  
   **Correct (any 3):** "Guests seeking bold, fruit-forward wines," "Those trying Australian wine," "Diners with grilled/spiced food," "First-time wine drinkers wanting approachability"  
   **Incorrect:** "Minimalist palate seekers," "Champagne-only regulars"

2. **Prompt:** To upsell wine effectively, which three elements matter?  
   **Correct (any 3):** "Ask about food choice first," "Suggest by price point," "Tell a story about the wine," "Offer taste before committing"  
   **Incorrect:** "Always push highest margin," "Assume guest knows wine," "Ignore food pairing"

3. **Prompt:** When a wine tastes off/corky, which three actions should you take?  
   **Correct (any 3):** "Smell cork first to diagnose," "Offer immediate replacement," "Taste to confirm issue," "Apologize without defensiveness"  
   **Incorrect:** "Insist nothing is wrong," "Charge for replacement," "Suggest guest palate is wrong"

#### Level 4: Roleplay Scenarios
1. **Scenario:** A guest at the table is from a wine region (e.g., Napa Valley). They order wine. Walk through how you'd interact, balancing respect for their knowledge with your expertise.  
   **Evaluation:** Humility, confidence, product knowledge, ability to pivot conversation.

2. **Scenario:** A couple on a first date orders wine. One chose white, one red. Walk through service and conversation that makes both feel welcome and might lead to future visits.  
   **Evaluation:** Attentiveness, personalization, wine knowledge, memorable service.

3. **Scenario:** A guest orders a Cabernet at $80/bottle but the wine list has a $45 option that's better-reviewed. Walk through the recommendation without sounding pushy.  
   **Evaluation:** Product knowledge, tact, problem-solving, sales skill.

### MODULE 3: Cocktail Fundamentals
- **ID:** 3
- **Category:** Technical
- **Difficulty:** 3/5
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Description:** Cocktail technique, measurement, spirit knowledge, classic recipes, garnish, riffing.
- **Target Learner:** Bartender, Waiter
- **Scenarios Count:** 14 (3 L1, 4 L2, 4 L3, 3 L4)

#### Level 1: Rapid-Fire Quiz
1. **Q:** What is the standard jigger measurement for a cocktail spirit pour?  
   **A:** 1.5 oz (45ml) in Australia (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Australian standard is 30ml for spirit, plus mixers/modifiers totaling 45ml drink.

2. **Q:** When shaking a cocktail, how long should you shake?  
   **A:** 10-15 seconds (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Long enough to chill, dilute, and integrate ingredients without over-diluting.

3. **Q:** What is the proper ratio for a Margarita (spirit-forward cocktail)?  
   **A:** 2 parts spirit, 1 part citrus, 1 part liqueur (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Classic 2:1:1 ratio; adjust to taste preference.

#### Level 2: Descriptor Selection
1. **Prompt:** A guest orders a Daiquiri. Which are correct preparation steps?  
   **Correct:** "Shake with ice, strain into coup glass," "Measure 1.5oz rum, 0.75oz lime, 0.5oz simple syrup"  
   **Incorrect:** "Stir in mixing glass, no ice," "Add Coke," "Serve over ice"

2. **Prompt:** When building a cocktail at the bar, which practices minimize waste and look professional?  
   **Correct:** "Pre-chill glasses when possible," "Batch similar cocktails to save ice/time"  
   **Incorrect:** "Free-pour without measuring," "Remix failed drinks back into bottles," "Ignore guest preferences"

3. **Prompt:** A Negroni (stirred, spirit-forward) should be served:  
   **Correct:** "Over ice in a rocks glass," "With orange peel garnish"  
   **Incorrect:** "Shaken into coup glass," "With cherry garnish," "Blended"

4. **Prompt:** Which garnishes enhance flavor vs. purely visual?  
   **Correct:** "Citrus zest oils flavor the drink," "Fresh herbs like mint impart taste"  
   **Incorrect:** "Cherry is purely decorative," "Umbrella adds flavor," "Straws matter to taste"

#### Level 3: Advanced Descriptors
1. **Prompt:** A guest asks for a "smooth whiskey cocktail that isn't a Manhattan." Which three options fit?  
   **Correct (any 3):** "Old Fashioned," "Sazerac," "Smash (whiskey, mint, fruit)," "Whiskey Sour"  
   **Incorrect:** "Margarita," "Mojito"

2. **Prompt:** To create a seasonal cocktail riff on a classic Margarita, which three modifications work?  
   **Correct (any 3):** "Swap lime for passion fruit," "Add fresh herbs like basil," "Use aged spirits instead of blanco," "Infuse sugar syrup with spice"  
   **Incorrect:** "Remove spirit entirely," "Add cola," "Use grocery-store mix"

3. **Prompt:** When a guest requests a custom cocktail based on their flavor profile, which are pro moves?  
   **Correct (any 3):** "Ask about spirit preference first," "Suggest structure (spirit-forward vs fruity)," "Offer to make a taster first," "Build on classic ratios you know work"  
   **Incorrect:** "Guess wildly," "Use every bottle on the shelf," "Charge extra for customization without reason"

#### Level 4: Roleplay Scenarios
1. **Scenario:** A guest at your bar has never had a cocktail. Walk through how you'd recommend one, build it, and explain what makes it special.  
   **Evaluation:** Education, confidence, attention to technique, hospitality.

2. **Scenario:** Two bartenders on shift; you're covered in orders. Describe your prioritization, batch-making strategy, and how you maintain quality under pressure.  
   **Evaluation:** Time management, consistency, communication, professionalism.

3. **Scenario:** A guest says their cocktail tastes "off"—maybe too strong, too sweet, too bitter. Walk through your diagnostic and fix without making them feel bad.  
   **Evaluation:** Problem-solving, empathy, technical skill, service recovery.

### MODULE 4: Coffee/Barista Basics
- **ID:** 4
- **Category:** Technical
- **Difficulty:** 2/5
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Description:** Espresso extraction, milk steaming, latte/cappuccino ratios, grind consistency, temperature, customer preferences.
- **Target Learner:** Bartender, Waiter, Bar Back
- **Scenarios Count:** 12 (3 L1, 3 L2, 3 L3, 3 L4)

#### Level 1: Rapid-Fire Quiz
1. **Q:** What is the proper ratio of espresso to milk for a cappuccino?  
   **A:** 1:1:1 (espresso:steamed milk:foam) (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Australian tradition; creates balance between coffee and milk.

2. **Q:** What temperature should milk be steamed to?  
   **A:** 60-65°C (140-150°F) (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Hot enough to dissolve sugar, cool enough to avoid scalding flavor.

3. **Q:** What does "pulling a shot" mean in espresso?  
   **A:** Forcing hot water through ground coffee under pressure (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Creates espresso; takes 25-30 seconds for optimal extraction.

#### Level 2: Descriptor Selection
1. **Prompt:** A customer orders a "strong coffee." Which options might satisfy?  
   **Correct:** "Double espresso," "Americano with extra shot"  
   **Incorrect:** "Flat white (standard)," "Iced latte," "Decaf"

2. **Prompt:** When steaming milk, which techniques create proper microfoam?  
   **Correct:** "Insert wand 1/3 below surface," "Maintain whirlpool motion"  
   **Incorrect:** "Blow hard at surface," "Keep wand fully submerged," "No movement needed"

3. **Prompt:** A guest says their coffee tastes bitter. What might have caused it?  
   **Correct:** "Over-extraction (shot pulled too long)," "Beans too finely ground"  
   **Incorrect:** "Milk too warm," "Not enough espresso," "Cup too large"

#### Level 3: Advanced Descriptors
1. **Prompt:** To troubleshoot espresso machine issues, which three steps diagnose problems?  
   **Correct (any 3):** "Check grind consistency first," "Observe extraction speed (should be 25-30sec)," "Taste shots to confirm bitterness/under-extraction," "Verify water temperature"  
   **Incorrect:** "Always use finer grind," "Tamp harder"

2. **Prompt:** A morning rush hits; you have 20 coffee orders in 10 minutes. Which three practices help?  
   **Correct (any 3):** "Batch similar drinks (all lattes, then cappuccinos)," "Pre-steam milk for multiple cups," "Use grinder ahead of rush," "Communicate with team"  
   **Incorrect:** "Skip milk steaming quality," "Use instant espresso," "Rush milk temperature checks"

3. **Prompt:** A regular customer always orders a flat white. Which three should be your service standards?  
   **Correct (any 3):** "Remember their preference," "Double-check milk temperature," "Offer seasonal variations," "Thank them for repeat business"  
   **Incorrect:** "Assume they always want the same," "Don't engage in conversation," "Charge inconsistently"

#### Level 4: Roleplay Scenarios
1. **Scenario:** A customer orders a flat white but you notice they're in a hurry. Walk through your service—speed without sacrificing quality.  
   **Evaluation:** Attentiveness, technical skill, hospitality, time awareness.

2. **Scenario:** A guest complains their cappuccino has too much foam. Walk through your diagnosis and remake without defensiveness.  
   **Evaluation:** Problem-solving, customer service, technical correction, grace under pressure.

3. **Scenario:** A team member is new to espresso machines and keeps over-extracting shots. As a senior staff member, how do you coach them during a shift?  
   **Evaluation:** Leadership, patience, technical knowledge, teamwork.

### MODULE 5: Carrying Glassware & Trays
- **ID:** 5
- **Category:** Technical
- **Difficulty:** 1/5
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Description:** Proper grip, balance, tray technique, weight distribution, spill prevention, safe handling under pressure.
- **Target Learner:** Waiter, Bartender, Bar Back
- **Scenarios Count:** 10 (3 L1, 2 L2, 2 L3, 3 L4)

#### Level 1: Rapid-Fire Quiz
1. **Q:** What is the proper way to carry a single cocktail glass?  
   **A:** By the stem or base, never by the bowl (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Prevents fingerprints, maintains temperature, avoids breaking.

2. **Q:** When carrying multiple glasses on a tray, how should they be arranged?  
   **A:** Heaviest glasses toward center, lighter toward edges (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Maintains balance and reduces tipping risk.

3. **Q:** What is the maximum safe weight to carry on a service tray?  
   **A:** 10-15 lbs depending on tray size and personal strength (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Heavy loads increase drop risk; split multiple trips if needed.

#### Level 2: Descriptor Selection
1. **Prompt:** Carrying a tray of 8 glasses, which practices prevent spills?  
   **Correct:** "Hold tray at waist level with bent elbow," "Keep tray balanced against your body"  
   **Incorrect:** "Carry tray high above head," "Fill glasses to rim," "Carry with straight arm"

2. **Prompt:** A guest stands up just as you're arriving with drinks. What's your best move?  
   **Correct:** "Pause and wait for them to settle," "Approach from the side they're not moving to"  
   **Incorrect:** "Push forward confidently," "Place drinks without checking guest position," "Ask them to sit down"

#### Level 3: Advanced Descriptors
1. **Prompt:** During a busy service, you're carrying a full tray and a guest flags you down. Which three are safe options?  
   **Correct (any 3):** "Acknowledge them, finish delivery, return for their request," "Politely ask them to wait 30 seconds," "Use hand signal to flag nearby staff to help"  
   **Incorrect:** "Stop mid-delivery to assist," "Ignore them," "Place full tray on guest table to attend to them"

2. **Prompt:** You nearly drop a tray of drinks. What's the professional recovery?  
   **Correct (any 3):** "Regain balance calmly," "Apologize to guests if any concern," "Continue service without making a scene," "Check drinks before serving to ensure quality"  
   **Incorrect:** "Panic visibly," "Blame someone else," "Serve drinks you're unsure about," "Make jokes about being clumsy"

#### Level 4: Roleplay Scenarios
1. **Scenario:** You're carrying 8 cocktails during Friday night rush. Describe your approach to the table, hand-off, and how you ensure each guest gets the right drink.  
   **Evaluation:** Safety, accuracy, grace under pressure, communication.

2. **Scenario:** A child runs in front of you while you're carrying a tray. Walk through what happens—safety first.  
   **Evaluation:** Risk assessment, quick thinking, professionalism, composure.

3. **Scenario:** You spill a drink on a guest's clothing. Walk through your recovery—apology, compensation offer, making them feel cared for.  
   **Evaluation:** Empathy, professionalism, service recovery, grace.

#### Level 4: Roleplay Scenarios

### MODULE 8: The Art of the Greeting
- **ID:** 8
- **Category:** Service
- **Difficulty:** 1/5
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Description:** First impression, warmth, tone of voice, body language, speed of greeting, personalization, anticipating needs.
- **Target Learner:** Waiter, Bartender, Host
- **Scenarios Count:** 10 (3 L1, 3 L2, 2 L3, 2 L4)

#### Level 1: Rapid-Fire Quiz
1. **Q:** What is the ideal time to greet a table after they sit?  
   **A:** Within 1-2 minutes (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Shows attentiveness; allows them time to settle but not feel ignored.

2. **Q:** What is the proper greeting posture?  
   **A:** Stand at the side of the table, open body language, smile (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Approachable, warm, not looming over or backing away.

3. **Q:** What should your first sentence always include?  
   **A:** "Welcome" or name acknowledgment + offer to help (correct)  
   **Explanation:** Sets tone of hospitality and availability.

#### Level 2: Descriptor Selection
1. **Prompt:** A couple comes in on their anniversary. Which are welcoming first moves?  
   **Correct:** "Warmly welcome them," "Ask if this is a special occasion"  
   **Incorrect:** "Immediately push drinks," "Ignore the context," "Treat them like any guest"

2. **Prompt:** A regular customer walks in during a busy shift. How do you greet them?  
   **Correct:** "Acknowledge them by name if you remember," "Show genuine pleasure at seeing them"  
   **Incorrect:** "Ignore them during your task," "Forget their usual," "Make them feel like one of the crowd"

3. **Prompt:** A group of rowdy friends arrives. What's the right tone?  
   **Correct:** "Upbeat and matching their energy," "Set expectations kindly about noise/behavior"  
   **Incorrect:** "Stuffy and formal," "Immediately warn them about noise," "Assume trouble"

#### Level 3: Advanced Descriptors
1. **Prompt:** To make greetings feel personal without being intrusive, which three apply?  
   **Correct (any 3):** "Remember regulars' names and drinks," "Notice context (celebration, business meeting)," "Use open-ended questions (not yes/no)," "Show genuine interest, not robotic"

2. **Prompt:** You overhear a first date. What's the right greeting approach?

   **Incorrect:** "Make comments about them being on a date," "Stay hovering," "Rush them to order"

#### Level 4: Roleplay Scenarios
1. **Scenario:** A large group of tourists walks in—they're clearly from out of town and a bit overwhelmed. Walk through your greeting and how you'd make them feel welcomed.
